First, higher energy prices will raise the cost of running AI infrastructure , reducing projected returns. Training and running AI models is notoriously energy-intensive, with energy estimated to account for between 15% and 25% of data centre operating costs. Second, higher energy prices will also push up the cost of producing hardware for data centres , which could likewise weigh on returns. The big exporters of AI-related equipment, Korea and Taiwan, are significant net energy importers and therefore particularly vulnerable to higher prices.
