## Investment Implications

## Judgement

This is the broad portfolio stance implied by the current corpus.

### Prefer

- Treat selective risk as the base case rather than broad risk-on beta.
- Keep duration tactical and be suspicious of complacent long-end comfort.
- Separate AI infrastructure and quality growth from indiscriminate equity exposure.
- Respect health care and other quality defensives more than deep cyclical beta.
- Underwrite private credit carefully and distrust concentrated software or hyperscaler exposure.
- Watch oil, the dollar, and EM flows together because that is where cross-asset pressure should reappear first.

### Be Careful With

- long-dated sovereign duration as a default safe haven
- weak software, narrow mega-cap concentration, and easy-multiple thinking
- deep cyclical beta that assumes the energy shock fades quickly
- concentrated private-credit books tied to software or hyperscaler risk
- broad EM beta if dollar and oil pressure accelerate together

### What Looks Most Important Right Now

- whether the energy shock fades or starts to embed into a second inflation wave
- whether bond supply and term-premia pressure keep duration from behaving as a hedge
- whether AI capex and leadership stay resilient enough to justify select tech exposure
- whether concentrated private-credit stress stays contained or starts to spread
- whether dollar strength and EM outflows are stabilising or worsening

## Counter-Case

- The current corpus may still over-represent a de-escalation baseline or one research-house framing.
- Cross-asset relationships can break if the geopolitical shock changes shape rather than simply fading.
- Some of the strongest evidence surfaces are still research notes rather than first-party market data series.

## Bottom Line

The corpus currently supports a stance of selective risk rather than broad risk-on positioning. Respect the inflation and duration problem; do not assume the long end has become easy again. Treat AI as structurally alive but internally differentiated, with a higher bar for weak software and narrow multiple expansion. Treat private credit as an opportunity set that now requires real skepticism about concentration and underwriting quality.
